About the Islamic Calendar

The Islamic calendar (Hijri calendar) is a lunar calendar consisting of 12 months in a year of 354 or 355 days. It is used to determine the proper days of Islamic holidays and rituals, such as the annual fasting and the annual season for the great pilgrimage.

The Islamic calendar began in 622 CE during the emigration (Hijrah) of Prophet Muhammad ï·º from Makkah to Madinah. Each month begins with the sighting of the new moon, making the Islamic calendar approximately 11 days shorter than the Gregorian solar calendar.

The 12 Islamic Months: Muharram, Safar, Rabi' al-Awwal, Rabi' al-Thani, Jumada al-Awwal, Jumada al-Thani, Rajab, Sha'ban, Ramadan, Shawwal, Dhul Qi'dah, Dhul Hijjah.
